Tiger 131

The Tiger tank, formally known as the Panzerkampfwagen VI Tiger Ausf. E, stands as one of the most feared and formidable armored vehicles of World War II. Introduced by Germany in 1942, the Tiger was characterized by its imposing size, thick armor, and powerful 88mm main gun, which could penetrate enemy armor with devastating effect from considerable distances. With its sloped armor design, the Tiger offered enhanced protection against incoming fire, while its advanced suspension system facilitated superior mobility despite its considerable weight. However, this formidable combination of firepower, armor, and mobility came at a cost, as the Tiger's complex engineering and maintenance requirements often strained German logistics and production capacities. Despite its limited numbers and logistical challenges, the Tiger earned a fearsome reputation on the battlefield, striking fear into the hearts of Allied tank crews and earning its place as one of the most iconic symbols of German armored prowess during World War II.
